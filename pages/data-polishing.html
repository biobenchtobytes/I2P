Polishing

Cleaned data may require additional polishing.

This might involve combining datasets from multiple sources, transforming, and imputing or removing missing values.

Combining

Dataframes can be combined along the vertical axis using concat().

df1 = pd.DataFrame({'A': ['purple', 'red', 'orange'], 
                    'B': [1, 2, 3], 
                    'C': ['C0', 'C1', 'C2'],
                    'D': ['dinosaur', 'drain', 'draw']})
df1
A B C D
0 purple 1 C0 dinosaur
1 red 2 C1 drain
2 orange 3 C2 draw 
df2 = pd.DataFrame({'A': ['green', 'blue', 'yellow'], 
                    'B': [4, 5, 6], 
                    'C': ['C0', 'C1', 'C2'],
                    'E': ['elephant', 'empty', 'easel']})
df2
A B C E
0 green 4 C0 elephant
1 blue 5 C1 empty
2 yellow 6 C2 easel 
# Concatenate the two DataFrames
result = pd.concat([df1, df2])
result
A B C D E
0 purple 1 C0 dinosaur NaN
1 red 2 C1 drain NaN
2 orange 3 C2 draw NaN
0 green 4 C0 NaN elephant
1 blue 5 C1 NaN empty
2 yellow 6 C2 NaN easel

Dataframes can be combined along the horizontal axis using merge().

# Merge the two DataFrames along rows
merged = pd.merge(df1, df2, on='C')
merged
A_x B_x C D A_y B_y E
0 purple 1 C0 dinosaur green 4 elephant
1 red 2 C1 drain blue 5 empty
2 orange 3 C2 draw yellow 6 easel 
# Merge the two DataFrames along rows
merged = pd.merge(df1, df2, on='C', suffixes=['_df1', '_df2'])
merged
A_df1 B_df1 C D A_df2 B_df2 E
0 purple 1 C0 dinosaur green 4 elephant
1 red 2 C1 drain blue 5 empty
2 orange 3 C2 draw yellow 6 easel

Transforming

Datasets often need to be transformed between wide and long form data.

Pandas provides this functionality in the form of pd.pivot for long to wide:

pd.pivot_table(
        proteins, 
        index='Subcellular Location', 
        columns=['Function'], 
        values='Molecular Weight (kDa)' 
              )
NoteThe equivalent function for wide to long is pd.melt.
